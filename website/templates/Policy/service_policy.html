SAFFO SOLUTION TECHNOLOGY

Service Rules

Office Timings

• General office timings are 9:30 AM to 6:00 PM, with a lunch break of 30 minutes; to be taken between 1:00 PM and 1:30 PM. As per the policy, if the employee comes up to 9:35 AM, he / she will be considered as having arrived on time, and this will be applicable for the whole month. After 9:35 AM, if the employee is late for three days, he / she will be marked absent for a day. Thereafter, for every day he/she arrives after 9:35 AM, he/she will be marked absent for half day. As per company policy, every employee has to complete 8.30 hours daily is compulsory.

• The working days for Admin, Sales and Operations Department will be from Monday – Saturday.

• The working days for Engineering will be from Monday – Friday and Saturday will be working as and when demand arises.

• Employee has to complete a minimum of 4 hours and 30 minutes for half day. Less than those hours will be treated as absent.

• The above are general office timings. In case the organization feels that some employees need to have a different set of office timings because of professional requirements, a specific timing can be separately given by his reporting officer.

Attendance and Punctuality

• In case there is no communication from employee on absence neither he/she is contactable for 5 days in continuation it will be considered as unauthorized absence and strict disciplinary action will be taken against the employee.

• It is the basic responsibility of the Reporting Manager for monitoring employee attendance and take appropriate actions.

• Time and attendance must be managed rightly. An employee is expected to be punctual and be available at work station before the start of office timings.

• Employees coming to the office late will be marked late as per the time and attendance policy, and they must inform HR and the Reporting Manager the reason for coming late.

Probation Period

• There is a six month’s probation period for all, and no paid leave will be given in probation. You can take an optional leave only after the completion of your probation period. (Refer to the list of leaves for more details.)

• Your confirmation will be based on the following factors:

▪ Performance
▪ Number of leaves taken in the probation period
▪ Code of conduct

Leave

• All employees are entitled to 8 days of casual leave (CL) and 13 days of privilege leave (PL) per year after the completion of the probation period.

1. Privilege leaves are provided for planned long leaves for the purpose of travel, vacation, etc. These leaves are earned on monthly basis and the unused leaves can be carried forward to next one year only.

2. Causal leaves are for the certain unforeseen situation; the employee can take one- or two days leaves. These leaves are not carried forward.

Kindly note only Privilege Leaves can be utilized for long leaves.

• The employee is eligible to take 1.75 leaves every month after successful completion of the probation.

• Employees completing the probation period between the 1st and 15th of a month will be eligible for paid leave from that particular month.

• Employees completing the probation period between the 16th and 30th of a month will be eligible for paid leave from next month onwards.

• While opting for optional leave, employees must mail 2 days in advance to the HR department and the manager.

• It is mandatory for all the employees to have their leaves sanctioned by the Reporting Manager at least two days in advance. Employees must apply for leave via mail. In rare cases, when the prior mail is not possible, employees should get approval via mail within 2 days of rejoining. In case of any emergency, you are required to inform about your absence along with the reason for absence to the Reporting Manager and HR department. No late leave petitions will be entertained, especially after the month end.

• Leave can be taken for even a half day at a time. As a professional, it is expected that employees would inform their Reporting Manager in advance if they expect to take a day or half-day off. Leaving messages with the receptionist or colleagues is not acceptable.

• In ‘Leave Management', there is a concept of 'Prefix/ Suffix'. Normally, company permit Sundays/ Closed Holidays to be either prefixed or suffixed with leave, but not both. For example, if an employee takes leave on Monday, Sunday can be permitted as a prefix. And if an employee is on leave on Saturday, Sunday can be given as a suffix. If the employee is on leave on Saturday and also on Monday, then in that case, the employee has to be on leave from Saturday to Monday (3 days) since he/she can't have the privilege of having Sunday, both as a prefix & suffix.

• Employees willing to use their comp-off during long leave will be either can take starting or ending of leave period. Can not be adjusted to middle days of leave period.

• The leave entitlement runs from January to December. It will be pro-rata for employees joining after January 1st.

• When there will be travel (Local/International) on weekends (Saturday & Sunday) then Saturday will not be considered for compensatory off against those weekends.

• When there will be a continuation for on-going FTs (Local/International) more than 5 days.

which includes Saturday & Sunday then the following should be taken into consideration from case to case:

▪ If there is a holiday approved by the client for weekends/weekdays for on-going FTs then those days will be considered regular holidays, which will be adjusted with your weekend, i.e., Sunday only.

▪ For the engineering team, during on-going FTs or retests assigned by the Test Lead/ Reporting Manager, Saturday will be a full working day.

▪ In case you are doing any FTs in your respective locations, which will be your local base/office/home/hotel then only Sunday will be considered for leave against those weekends work.

• Its mandatory for an engineer to take leave against working Sunday and public holidays in the following week, with prior approval from his Reporting Manager.

• Privileged leave (PL) can be accumulated/carried forward for a period of one year.

• There is no facility for leave encashment.

Overtime

• You are also required to work overtime in addition to your normal hours of work, if required for the proper performance of your duties.

• You will not receive any additional remuneration for hours worked in excess of your normal working hours.

Office Housekeeping

• Away from home, some people do not worry about their housekeeping habits. Tardiness or sloppiness, if present, is visible at the office and has an immediate impact on visitors. It is automatic to associate an office in disarray with carelessness at work. No files and other material must be kept scattered at the work place. Similarly, Tea–Coffee spills, used cups, and food particles all over the work desk present a poor image.

Transferability

• The organization will have the inherent power to transfer any employee to any location where it is felt that the services of the employee would be suitable for the organization.

Social Security

• It is prohibited to upload content without consent. Therefore, sharing unauthorized content on social media without permission violates confidentiality and should be avoided.

• Employees are not supposed to post anything confidential or not yet published.

• Here are some examples of what should not be posted on social media: posting commentary, content, or images that are defamatory, proprietary, harassing, libelous, or can create a hostile work environment.

Confidentiality

• Employees are expected to maintain the confidentiality of company information and not share it with unauthorized individual.

IT Cyber Security

• Employees with access to the company’s assets, login system, and technology are to adhere to the terms of the policy.

• It is the obligation of all users of the company systems to protect the technology and information assets of the company. This information must be protected from unauthorized access, theft and destruction. The technology and information assets of the company are made up of the following components:

▪ Mobile handsets, Laptop, watches, headphones, Computer hardware, CPU, disc, Email, web, application servers, PC systems, application software, system software, etc.
▪ System Software including: operating systems, database management systems, and backup and restore software, communications protocols, and so forth.
▪ Application Software: used by the various departments within the company. This includes custom written software applications, and commercial off the shelf software packages.
▪ Communications Network hardware and software including: routers, routing tables, hubs, modems, multiplexers, switches, firewalls, private lines, and associated network management software and tools.

• If the breach is unintended and for the first time, then a verbal warning is given to the employee. He/ She is further educated about the importance of a company’s security.

• If the breach has a repeated pattern & is done intentionally, then strict and severe disciplinary action is taken against the employee who violated the policy. In extreme cases, the steps might lead to employee termination, especially when the company faces severe financial loss or other significant damages due to an employee’s non-adherence to the policy.

ID Card Policy

• The employees shall wear their ID cards AT ALL TIMES in a visible manner when they are on the company premises. This is a MANDATORY requirement.

• The employees who repeatedly fail to wear the ID card or declines to wear the ID cards are said to breach this policy’s provisions and shall face disciplinary actions.

• The Reporting Managers or the colleagues are expected to remind the employees to wear the ID card if they are not wearing it.

• The employee who lends his/her ID card to anyone else (whether they are an employee of the organization) to get an entry on the company premises shall face legal actions.

Lost or Stolen ID Card

• Suppose the employee discovers that his/her ID card is stolen or lost; in that case, they must report the same to the HR Department via email immediately because this can be a threat to the organization’s security.

• If unreported, the employee shall be held responsible for all the activities undertaken using their ID card.

• Replacement for misplacing or losing the ID card will cause the employee to be charged rupees Rs. 500 for each substitution.

• If any employee finds a lost ID card, they must return it to HR Department.

Loan/Advance Payment

• The employee is eligible for taking the loan or advance payment only after successfully completing employment of 2 years.

Company Assets

• If any company assets are lost while on tour the same should be informed to the concerned authority immediately. In case no action taken company reserves the right to recover the amount of lost assets from employee salary.

• If the company assets are damaged due to personal mistake, the total cost will be recovered from employee’s salary.
